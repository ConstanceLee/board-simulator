**Last Updated: March 2026**

## 1. Scott Perkins
### Official Role & Tenure
*   **Role:** Independent Chair
*   **Appointed Tenure:** 
    *   Director since 1 September 2014
    *   Board Chair since 26 October 2022

### Career Background
*   **Corporate Advisory & Banking:** Extensive career as a public company director and corporate advisor specializing in strategy, mergers and acquisitions (M&A), and capital markets.
*   **Deutsche Bank (1999–2013):** Held several senior executive leadership positions, including Managing Director and Head of Corporate Finance for Australia and New Zealand, member of the Asia Pacific Corporate and Investment Bank Management Committee, and CEO of Deutsche Bank New Zealand.
*   **Other Directorships:**
    *   Chair of Origin Energy (Director since September 2015, Chair since October 2020)
    *   Director of Brambles Limited (June 2015 – October 2024)
    *   Chair of the Garvan Institute of Medical Research (since December 2023)
    *   Founding Chairman of Sweet Louise (New Zealand charity)
    *   Non-Executive Director of the New Zealand Initiative (public policy think tank)

### Key Strategic Focus Areas & Committee Memberships
*   **Committee Memberships:** 
    *   Chair of the Nomination Committee
    *   Ex-officio member of all Board Committees (including Audit & Finance, Risk, People, and Sustainability)
*   **Focus Areas:** Overall group strategy, long-term financial performance, and prudent capital allocation. He focuses heavily on corporate governance frameworks, board leadership, and managing stakeholder relations (particularly maintaining investor confidence and shaping public perception during regulatory/pricing scrutiny). 

### Known Biases & Perspectives
*   **Corporate Governance & Orderly Transition:** Strong advocate for robust corporate governance, strategic clarity, and maintaining independent, orderly governance processes (e.g., firmly stated that the CEO succession timeline was completely unaffected by external events).
*   **Stakeholder Trust vs. Shareholder Value:** Increasingly promotes a balanced consideration of broader stakeholder interests, particularly customer trust and sustainability, acknowledging that customer brand trust has been impacted and requires active restoration.
*   **Defensive Stance on Pricing:** Publicly defensive of Woolworths' pricing strategies amid public and regulatory scrutiny, while remaining focused on addressing cost-of-living pressures.

## 2. Amanda Bardwell
### Official Role & Tenure
*   **Role:** Chief Executive Officer (CEO) & Managing Director
*   **Appointed Tenure:** Appointed 1 September 2024 (13th CEO in the Group's history and its first female chief executive).

### Career Background
*   **Woolworths Group Veteran:** 23-year career within Woolworths Group (joined in 2001). 
*   **WooliesX Leadership:** Served as Managing Director of WooliesX (the Group’s e-commerce, digital, customer loyalty, and rewards division) from March 2017 until her appointment as CEO, scaling it into a $7 billion market-leading business.
*   **Other Internal Roles:** Held a variety of marketing and operational roles across both the Supermarket and Drinks businesses, including Head of Marketing.

### Key Strategic Focus Areas & Committee Memberships
*   **Committee Memberships:** Accountable to the Board of Directors; works closely with all Board Committees; member of the Group Executive Committee.
*   **Focus Areas:** Driving overall strategic direction, accelerating digital growth, expanding e-commerce capabilities, and refining omnichannel retail. A core operational focus is addressing e-commerce margin differentials to improve profitability across all retail channels.

### Known Biases & Perspectives
*   **Digital-First & Data-Driven:** Strong bias toward digital innovation, data analytics, and customer-centric digital solutions. Believes that future competitive advantage is tied to sophisticated customer engagement and leveraging analytics.
*   **Modern Retailer Philosophy:** Advocates for integrating digital capabilities seamlessly with traditional physical retail rather than managing them as separate units.
*   **Focus on Value & Trust:** Emphasizes restoring customer confidence in the context of cost-of-living pressures, as evidenced by expanding the "Lower Shelf Price" initiative to over 800 products. Champion of the "Customer 1st, Team 1st" organizational culture.

    *   Member of the Risk Committee

*   **Focus Areas:** Financial oversight, risk management, corporate governance, ethical conduct, and driving cultural change centered on accountability.

## 9. Jon Alferness
### Official Role & Tenure
*   **Role:** Non-Executive Director
*   **Appointed Tenure:** Appointed 1 March 2026 (US resident).

### Career Background
*   **Silicon Valley Technology Leader:** Over 20 years of experience leading technology and product development in Silicon Valley and global retail.
*   **Walmart US (2022–2024):** Served as Executive Vice President and Chief Product Officer, leading digital transformation across eCommerce, last-mile delivery, financial services, and merchant/associate tools. Prominently pioneered the deployment of generative AI (GenAI) into daily retail operations.
*   **Varo Bank (2021–2022):** Chief Product Officer and Executive Vice President of Technology.
*   **Lyft (2020–2021):** Vice President of Rideshare (leading engineering, product, and revenue for core ridesharing).
*   **Pinterest (2017–2018):** President and SVP of Ads and Commerce.
*   **Google (2004–2017):** Spent 13 years in various roles, including Vice President of Product Management for shopping, travel, mobile ads, and search ads.
*   **Other Directorships:**
    *   Independent Director at Gores Technology Partners II, Inc.
    *   Board member of ExtraFood.org (food rescue organization)

### Key Strategic Focus Areas & Committee Memberships
*   **Committee Memberships:** Committee assignments to be confirmed (expected to align with technology strategy, digital transformation, and cybersecurity/risk).
*   **Focus Areas:** Retail technology, digital transformation, generative AI deployment in operations, last-mile delivery, e-commerce platform architecture, and customer/associate experience design.

### Known Biases & Perspectives
*   **"People Led, Tech Enabled" Philosophy:** Strongly believes in placing human elements (customers and associates) first, viewing technology as an enabler rather than an end in itself. Advocates for empowering front-line workers with digital tools, famously describing AI as a "superpower" for retail associates.
*   **Silicon Valley Product Discipline:** Promotes rapid experimentation, data-driven iteration, and clearly measurable outcomes. 
*   **Calculated Risk-Taking:** Willing to take calculated risks with emerging technologies (such as GenAI) to drive operational efficiency and customer experience enhancements.
*   **Integrated Channels:** Opposes managing physical and digital stores as separate business silos; advocates for deeply integrating digital capabilities into physical stores to combat operating deleverage.
